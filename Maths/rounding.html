Learning Goal: To learn what rounding is and to be able to round numbers.

WHY WE ROUND NUMBERS

Rounding numbers is used to make them easier to read.

Some numbers have an infinite amount of decimals, and rounding them gives an approximation of what value the number holds. For example, ⅓ = 0.33. In reality, there is supposed to be an infinite number of 3’s, but we cannot write that many. Because of that, we have to round them.

HOW TO ROUND NUMBERS

When rounding a number, you will round it to an amount of “significant digits”. This means all numbers that are not 0, and all zeros that are between two non-zero digits (e.g. in 177013 all the digits are significant, but in 17701300 the final two digits are not significant, meaning that both numbers have 6 significant digits). Another way of rounding is by rounding to a certain amount, such as tenths (e.g. Rounding 12.72 up to the nearest tenth, which would equal 12.7).

A whole number is a number that does not have any decimal places

Round 17.7013 to the nearest hundredth

A hundredth is the second decimal place. To round 17.7013, we have to look at the number before 0, which is 1. Because 1 is lower than 5, we do not increase the value of the number after it (0) Then the unnecessary numbers after the hundredth are removed.
